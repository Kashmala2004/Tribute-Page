Early Life and Education

Nelson Mandela was born on July 18, 1918, in the small village of Mvezo in South Africa. He belonged to the Thembu royal family. As a child, he attended local Methodist schools and later studied law at the University of Fort Hare and the University of Witwatersrand.

Anti-Apartheid Activism

In 1944, Mandela joined the African National Congress (ANC) and co-founded its Youth League. He quickly became a key figure in the fight against apartheid — the system of racial segregation in South Africa. He led peaceful protests, defied unjust laws, and even organized national strikes. But as the government’s oppression grew, Mandela began advocating for armed resistance.

Imprisonment & Sacrifice

In 1962, he was arrested and sentenced to life in prison for conspiracy to overthrow the government. He spent 27 years in prison, including 18 years on Robben Island, often in harsh conditions. During this time, he became a powerful symbol of resistance and hope.

Freedom & Leadership

Mandela was released from prison in 1990, and four years later, in 1994, he became the first Black President of South Africa. His leadership helped unite a deeply divided nation and he worked tirelessly to promote reconciliation, peace, and equality.

Global Recognition

For his incredible efforts, Mandela received more than 250 honors, including the 1993 Nobel Peace Prize. He became a global icon for justice and human rights.

Legacy

Nelson Mandela passed away on December 5, 2013, at the age of 95. Today, he is remembered not only as South Africa’s “Father of the Nation” but as a universal symbol of freedom, dignity, and forgiveness.
